Project Description

"Univentures is a centralized web platform where University of Michigan students across disciplines can easily connect for the purpose of team building and idea generation."

This project initiated from my co-founder's personal painpoint of having difficulty in connecting with talents outside of our immediate network. University of Michigan is known for its passion and devotion for entrepreneurship with a lot of support from the school and interest from the students. However, while team building is one of the first, if not the first, barrier to encounter in launching a startup, there was no existing platform that enabled students to easily connect with each other.

We spent months reaching out not only to faculty members involved in various entrepreneurial programs across the campus including Tech Arb, Innovation in Action, Innovate Blue, Zell Lurie Institue, Center for Entrepreneurship, Desai Accelerator, but also student entrepreneurs to validate the idea and discuss the feasibility of the project. It didn't take long for us to realize that the painpoint was already widely known, and that a solution was in dire needs. We immediately jumped into wireframing and started developing a platform where students can connect with each other.

After launching the platform, we gained positive feedback with a number of students creating accounts on the platform. However, user traction stopped growing within a few weeks after the launch. After having a thorough discussion with our mentors, we realized that we had not conducted enough customer discovery. The platform was built based on our approximated guess and ideas as we did not have enough understanding of users' problems and needs. This was a turning point for us where we learned a valuable lesson, that thoroughly studying your users' needs is critical in building a product.

To build the 'right' product, we shut down the platform and decided to start from ground zero. We recruited 3 more team members with UX/UI Design background and started interviewing much wider and more variety group of user groups. We received feedback through multiple iterations of wireframing and comparative analysis which helped us readjust our goals. We're in the process of finalizing the wirefram and conducting usability testing.
